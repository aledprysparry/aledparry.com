## The Human Story

How do you encourage kids to put down their screens, lace up their boots and show up to grassroots football practice? That was the core problem posed by Hac Pêl-droed, an open hackathon competition launched by M-SParc in partnership with the Football Association of Wales (FAW).

Knowing that community engagement thrives on connection and gamification, I partnered with Ben Taylor and James Finney from 42able.ai, an AI-focused tenant based out of the M-SParc incubator. Together, we conceptualised and pitched a solution designed to make the daily grind of training fun. Our concept, Curiad, turned attendance into a game. By showing up to practice, young players earned digital points via an open API. These points could then be cashed in for coveted FAW merchandise or, crucially, kit and gear from their own local grassroots clubs.

## The Breakdown

**Type:** Hackathon Concept & Open API Architecture

**Role:** Co-Pitch, Product Designer & Creative Director

**The Challenge:** Designing an authentic, digital incentive loop for young people that actively promotes physical activity and deepens ties to local sports communities.

**The Solution:** A secure, scalable open-API platform blueprint that handles geo-location or coach-verified attendance tracking, converting physical presence into a universal digital reward currency.

**Deliverables:** Competition pitch deck, technical API integration framework, user journey wireframes, and rewards ecosystem architecture.

**The Impact:** The concept was shortlisted by the judging panel. While the top spot ultimately went to a physical product rather than our digital ecosystem, the project stood as a powerful, extensible proof of concept for modern sports gamification.
