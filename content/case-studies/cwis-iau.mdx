## The Human Story

Following the success of Cwis Bob Dydd, we ran into a frustrating compliance brick wall. Young audiences absolutely loved playing our daily quiz, but strict UK competition and advertising laws legally restrict us from targeting and onboarding users under the age of 16 when cash or physical prizes are involved. Prizes are the lifeblood of our user growth strategy, so this restriction felt like a double-edged sword.

We needed a completely safe, legal and educational environment where younger audiences could experience the joy of a daily quiz ritual. Enter Cwis-iau (a clever spin on Cwis and Iau / junior). Originally conceived during M-SParc's Hac Iaith, the concept won the hackathon outright. Backed by the Welsh Government's Cymraeg 2050 team, we turned that winning hack into a year-long, deep research project to adapt the core quiz architecture specifically for Welsh school classrooms.

## The Breakdown

**Type:** Educational Product Strategy & Compliance R&D

**Role:** Product Lead & Co-Creator

**The Challenge:** Navigating rigid data privacy and legal competition guardrails for minors while maintaining the competitive hook that makes daily digital quizzes successful.

**The Solution:** Re-engineering the competitive mechanic away from global, prize-led leaderboards into ring-fenced, classroom-safe educational brackets that gamify learning in the Welsh language.

**Deliverables:** Award-winning hackathon prototype, year-long educational feasibility report, compliance roadmap, and school-optimised gameplay framework.

**The Impact:** The research unlocked the school market, with a phased rollout of Cwis-iau officially entering classrooms across Wales starting in late 2026 and extending into 2027.
