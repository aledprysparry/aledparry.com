## The Human Story

This project was a beautifully complex jigsaw puzzle of international collaboration, cultural storytelling and multi-platform strategy. Building directly on the foundational partnership established by six County Councils across Wales and Ireland through the Celtic Routes tourism campaign, S4C commissioned us to bring this relationship to life on screen.

Co-produced alongside the Irish broadcaster TG4, the series followed musician Ryland Teifi and his two young daughters, Lowri and Cifa, as they travelled across the sea to rediscover their shared Celtic roots. The production brief was ambitious: the networks wanted three half-hour broadcast-quality television episodes, but we knew the younger demographic lived online. Adopting a glass-half-full mentality, our small agile crew engineered a digital-first production model. We shot the entire journey to deliver six mid-form YouTube episodes first, then seamlessly wrapped that content into the broadcast packages required by the TV networks.

## The Breakdown

**Type:** International TV Co-Production & Digital Series

**Role:** Producer & Digital Strategist

**The Challenge:** Maximising a lean budget to satisfy two major international television broadcasters while simultaneously building a native, high-engagement digital footprint for younger audiences.

**The Solution:** An agile, dual-purpose filming strategy utilising a small crew equipped for rapid setups, capturing both cinematic television content and native mid-form YouTube formats simultaneously.

**Deliverables:** 3 x 30-minute broadcast TV episodes (S4C & TG4), 6 x mid-form YouTube episodes, and an integrated digital tourism campaign asset kit.

**The Impact:** A premier showcase of resource pooling and cross-border partnership, proving that creative agility can deliver high-value, multi-platform storytelling without losing an ounce of human warmth.
